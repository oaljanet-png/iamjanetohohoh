The Journey

I've spent over 15 years on the retail floor — from H&M, where I led visual merchandising across 10 stores and achieved a global #1 performance ranking, to Louis Vuitton, Nespresso, Foot Locker, and Castlery. Across every role, I was the person who walked into a store, figured out what wasn't working, and fixed it.

I've redesigned KPI systems, overhauled stock-takes from a week-long exercise down to hours, built training programmes that moved conversion rates by 5.5%, and led teams through record-breaking sales periods. I also led the operational preparation for the first Louis Vuitton chocolate pilot store in Southeast Asia.

Today I'm an Associate Trainer with the Singapore Institute of Retail Studies (SIRS), delivering programmes in Visual Merchandising and Retail Excellence — and a Visual Merchandising Consultant for the Enhanced Heartland Visual Merchandising Programme (EVMHP), working directly with neighbourhood retailers to improve their store presentation and commercial impact.

What I Believe

Small businesses are run by some of the hardest working people I've ever met. But hard work alone isn't enough when the systems aren't there — the SOPs, the buying decisions, the customer data, the team structure. These aren't complicated fixes. Small tweaks make big differences.

I've seen money leak because of simple gaps — the wrong stock quantities, unclear roles, a customer profile nobody sat down to define. These are fixable. And fixing them doesn't have to be expensive or overwhelming.

I also believe that learning should never feel scary. Many business owners didn't have great experiences in school, and that can make asking for help feel like admitting failure. It's not. Getting support is the smartest move you can make — and I make sure every session feels safe, practical, and worth your time.

I learn just as much from the people I work with as they do from me. Every shop owner I've met has taught me something. That's what keeps this work meaningful.
